Student Money

Met card

MMU offer all its students a pre-paid card that can be used to pay for university goods and services such as:

  • Printing
  • Food from University cafes
  • Books and technology from the student union or Blackwell’s bookshop
  • Art and stationary supplies
  • Sport and leisure activities

Upon activation of the card, £20 will be deposited into your account from the university. Whether you’re undergraduate, foundation or first year you’ll be eligible for a card. You will be able to top-up this card online.

Part-time jobs

Earning money whilst studying is an opportunity which most students would love as it can be flexible around their timetable. Students working for the university are limited to working no more than 16 hours per week. The aims are to provide more opportunities for the students and ensure equal access to employment. To find out more, contact Jobs4Students.

Student discounts

With an NUS Extra card, you are eligible for over 150 offers and discounts and the card itself only costs £12 for the year. Retailers such as Topshop, ASOS and Odeon are a part of these discounts. If you are a sushi lover, YoSushi offer a 25% discount. However, this is only worth the money if you use the card.

Student living costs

Transport

Unfortunately, public transport is essential for every student moving around a new city but there are ways to make your money stretch further. Manchester offers a wide choice of transport including buses, trains, trams and bikes!

If you are likely to be travelling home often or live far away applying for a 16-25 Railcard, an International Student Identity Card or a Young Persons Coach card will help to significantly reduce your travel costs. Make sure to research which banks are offering student accounts with railcards.

Buses are an easily accessible mode of transport as well as cheap and comfortable. Students most favourable way of travelling up and down Oxford Road is the £1 magic bus.

Studying

Buying course textbooks can be an unnecessary expense. Opt for the cheap alternative and go to the library or go online and buy them second-hand.

Food

In terms of food, make sure to get the most out of your money and make a shopping list before you enter the shop as this will stop you wandering. Cooking in groups can also help to reduce waste and bond with your flatmates.
